# Zoom In

You're switching from reasoning to execution. Pull-out should have produced what you need to start: a filled `plan.md` with the question, the change, and the disconfirm; reflections in the journal; parked ideas in `scratch/parking_lot.md`. Zoom-in is the commitment moment, not the place to start thinking.

- **Restate the question from your plan.** Read the Question section of `plan.md` aloud. If it isn't filled, back to `pull-out` — zoom-in is for executing thought-through ideas, not generating them.
- **Set a budget.** How many experiments, how many minutes, before you'll pull back out and reassess? Unbounded focus is drift in disguise.
- **Confirm the parking lot.** Pull-out should have captured other threads and ideas in `scratch/parking_lot.md`. Glance at it once so you know where to put new ideas that surface mid-flow.
- **Single-tasking from here.** If a new idea pops up mid-experiment, append one line to the parking lot and keep going. Don't break flow chasing every thought — that's what the next pull-out is for.

Then go.
