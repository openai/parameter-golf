KEY FINDINGS (Research-Backed)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. **LeakyReLU(0.5)^2 activation**: Confirmed -0.006 bpb free improvement (exp1)
   - Eliminates dead neurons, preserves relu² inductive bias
   - One-line change: F.leaky_relu(x, negative_slope=0.5).square()

2. **3x MLP + more layers**: Biggest single gain (-0.024 to -0.042 bpb)
   - 10L/3xMLP is optimal balance for 500 iters on 3070
   - 11L/3xMLP is even better but way over size with int8

3. **EMA without QAT is CATASTROPHIC**: +0.15-0.18 bpb degradation
   - EMA-averaged weights have different distributions
   - Must pair EMA with QAT (STE fake quantization during training)
   - Winning submissions always use EMA + int6 QAT + GPTQ-lite

4. **Cosine warmdown >> linear warmdown**: -0.005 bpb (exp7 vs exp4)
   - 0.5*(1+cos(π*progress)) gives smoother LR decay
   - Optimal warmdown ratio: ~35% of total steps (175/500)

5. **Tuned LR from winning configs helps significantly**:
   - matrix_lr: 0.04 → 0.025 (more stable)
   - muon_momentum: 0.95 → 0.99 (better Muon convergence)
   - grad_clip: 0.0 → 0.3 (stabilizes large models)
   - tied_embed_lr: 0.05 → 0.035

6. **16MB size constraint is the key bottleneck**:
   - The best models (10L+/3xMLP) produce 17-20MB int8 artifacts
   - Winning submissions use int6 quantization (6 bits/param)
   - int6+zlib compresses 26M params to ~15.5MB
