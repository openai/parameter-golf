  2. The Scylla result is less magical than it looks

  I diffed records/track_10min_16mb/2026-03-31_Scylla_FullGPTQ_XSA11_FA3_0.9485/
  train_gpt.py against PR #1060's train_gpt.py. The only code delta is
  tokenizer-metadata loading (.meta.npz file + a load_tokenizer_luts helper that
   understands non-SentencePiece vocabs). No architecture change. No new op, no
  new block, no new quantization, no new scheduler.

  The submission just swaps the tokenizer from SP1024 (PR #1060) to a 998-token
  TokenMonster vocab (candidate.vocab) that was pre-trained externally (pruned
  from TokenMonster's english-1024-clean-v1). The score jumps 1.1122 вҶ’ 0.9485 =
  вҲ’0.1637 BPB from tokenizer alone.

  There are two reasons this is less of a "jump" than it looks:

  1. BPB is mechanical вҖ” BPB = CE_nats / bytes_per_token / ln 2. A tokenizer
  that packs more bytes per token lowers BPB proportionally, even at identical
  cross-entropy. TokenMonster's "ungreedy multi-branch" tokenization achieves
  higher bytes-per-token than SentencePiece BPE at similar vocab sizes. The
  model doesn't get "smarter"; each mistake just costs fewer bits per byte.
  2. The tokenizer was trained with external compute (TokenMonster's vocab
  construction is an offline optimization over a large corpus). This is allowed
  under the challenge rules for tokenizer artifacts but it's very clearly a case
   of spending training compute elsewhere.

  So the right question isn't "can we match Scylla." It's: how much cheap BPB 
  can we buy with an SP4096 or SP8192 tokenizer swap (which is pedestrian,
  documented, and already done by #1218/#1394/#1493), and separately, should we
  invest in producing our own TokenMonster-class vocab before the 2026-04-30
  deadline (5 days left).

  4. Adoption priority вҖ” ranked by marginal BPB per engineering hour

  Working from our 1.1117 baseline with 5 days until 2026-04-30. Each item is a
  delta we can isolate from the upstream record logs.

  Tier 1 вҖ” cheap, documented, high expected gain

  A. Tokenizer swap to SP4096 or SP8192.
  - PR #1218 (SP4096) вҶ’ 1.0979 from 1.1194 baseline on the same architecture =
  вҲ’0.0215 BPB.
  - PR #1394 (SP8192) вҶ’ 1.0856, which is вҲ’0.033 BPB over our 1.1117.
  - Cost: retokenizing FineWeb once (~60вҖ“90 min on a single H100), + ~50KB in
  the tokenizer model in the 16MB budget. Use
  data/download_hf_docs_and_tokenize.py with vocab_size=4096 or 8192, or
  download @clarkkev's pre-tokenized dataset from kevclark/parameter-golf on HF.
   The infrastructure works today; PR #1218 documents it fully.
  - Risk: embedding matrix grows from (1024,В 512) to (4096/8192,В 512), eating
  ~3MB of 16MB. Both top records solve this by GPTQ-quantizing the embedding at
  int8.
  - Expected at our stack: somewhere in вҲ’0.015 to вҲ’0.030 BPB, probably landing
  us around 1.08вҖ“1.09 before any other change.

  B. MuonEq-R (row-normalize gradients before Newton-Schulz).
  - Implementation: 2 lines in Muon.step, right before
  zeropower_via_newtonschulz5(...):
  row_norms = g.float().norm(dim=-1, keepdim=True).clamp_min(1e-7)
  g = g / row_norms.to(g.dtype)
  - Gated by a row_normalize=True flag on the Muon param groups.
  - Quoted cost per PR #1285: "Zero-byte cost, ~0.001 BPB improvement." Per PR
  #1334 composition: part of вҲ’0.008 BPB over PR #1218.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.001 to вҲ’0.002 BPB, no risk.

  C. SDClip replaces percentile search.
  - Our quantize_int6_gptq currently sweeps [0.999, 0.9995, 0.9999, 0.99999, 
  1.0] per matrix picking min MSE. PR #1394 replaces this with clip = k В· 
  std(row) where k=12.85 for int6 matrices and k=20 for int8 embeddings. The k
  values are tuned once so the artifact lands close to 16MB; they don't need
  per-run search.
  - Gain: the PR's mathematical justification (H(q) вүҲ b - logвӮӮ(k) + const) shows
   increasing k is equivalent to dropping a bit вҖ” so choosing k to target a
  specific compressed size is principled and gives better recon-per-byte than
  per-row MSE search.
  - Cost: saves the percentile-search inner loop (our code runs GPTQ once per
  candidate, wasting ~4Г— the budget). Faster, simpler, and directly controls
  artifact size.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.001 BPB from better quantization + more artifact
  headroom to keep every layer at int6 (relevant to item E below).

  D. Brotli-11 + byte-shuffle instead of LZMA-9.
  - PR #1089 (original), PR #1179 (merged), PR #1218 (confirmed). Byte-shuffle
  reorders the int6 tensor bytes such that high-order bits are contiguous,
  improving the entropy profile Brotli sees.
  - Cost: ~50 lines of Python; pip install brotli; decoder is ~10 lines in the
  self-extracting wrapper.
  - Gain quoted: ~5% smaller artifact vs LZMA on int6-quantized tensors. That's
  ~800KB of headroom we can spend on something else (bigger embeddings, more
  layers int6 instead of int5).
  - Expected at our stack: indirect вҖ” on its own maybe вҲ’0.001 BPB, but enables
  (E) below.

  E. All-layer int6 via higher WD + SDClip + Brotli headroom.
  - PR #1285's insight: bumping WD from 0.085 вҶ’ 0.090 shrinks matrix L2 norms вҶ’
  shrinks brotli size ~5% вҶ’ artifact headroom lets you keep all 66 layers at
  int6 (vs 60вҖ“61 int6 with 5вҖ“6 int5 demotions). Quoted gain: 1.0917 вҶ’ 1.0912 =
  вҲ’0.0005 BPB, small but free once (A)+(C)+(D) are in.
  - Expected at our stack: probably not measurable alone; stacks with (A)(C)(D).

  F. QK-Gain init 2.5 вҶ’ 5.0 (or 5.25).
  - Single env var. PR #1493 reports monotonic improvement from 4.0 вҶ’ 5.25 on
  their stack. PR #1413 attributes ~вҲ’0.003 BPB to jumping 4.0 вҶ’ 5.0.
  - Caveat: the optimum depends on the overall stack; at our SP1024 setup, 2.5
  was tuned. But 2.5 looks too conservative compared to the wider community
  consensus of 5.0. Worth a 3-seed sweep at {2.5, 4.0, 5.0, 5.25}.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.001 to вҲ’0.003 BPB.

  Tier 2 вҖ” structural but small, well-documented patches

  G. MLP multiplier 3Г— вҶ’ 4Г— (+ WD 0.085).
  - PR #1218's headline change: simplifies everything else (removes TTT, QAT,
  gated attention, value residuals, hash embeddings, smear gate) and the
  remaining bigger-model-with-higher-WD goes from 1.1194 вҶ’ 1.0979 = вҲ’0.0215 BPB.
   Chunk of this is the SP4096 swap, but even in-domain the MLP 4x change is
  non-negative.
  - Cost: config change, but doubles MLP parameters and may push artifact over
  16MB without (C)(D)(E). Do this after tokenizer + SDClip + Brotli.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.005 to вҲ’0.010 BPB assuming it fits.

  H. Depth recurrence on layers 4вҖ“5 (Г— 2) activated at 50%.
  - PR #1285 and #1334 wire this through encoder_indices/decoder_indices lists
  built at model init. The forward pass toggles between linear and looped
  iteration at step = enable_looping_at * total_iterations. Shares the
  MLP+attention weights but the recurrence is only on layers 4,5 (and PR #1493
  extends to 3,4,5 for the 3-layer version).
  - Trade-off: each recurrence pass adds a full block-time; PR #1204 reports
  that recurring 3 layers already loses to step-time penalty.
  - Gain: PR #1285 reports "~0.003 BPB improvement" for layers 4,5 looped once.
  PR #1493's 3-layer variant is ~вҲ’0.002 BPB on top of 2-layer.
  - Implementation cost: ~40 lines in GPT.__init__ + forward_logits + a
  scheduler hook. Our train_gpt_swa.py:839-900 has a flat block iteration вҖ”
  refactor to drive by encoder_indices/decoder_indices.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.002 to вҲ’0.004 BPB.

  I. Parallel residuals from layer 7.
  - Two variants:
    - PR #1412 (simpler, non-record but well-tested): same input to both attn
  and MLP, both add to same residual.
    - PR #1204/#1477 (more capacity): separate residual lanes with learnable
  lane_merge init 0.5.
  - Gain per PR #1204: ~0.002 BPB. Per PR #1477: this + score-first TTT composes
   additively.
  - Implementation: ~20 lines in Block.forward conditional on layer_idx >= 
  PARALLEL_START_LAYER.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.001 to вҲ’0.003 BPB.

  Tier 3 вҖ” bigger ROI but heavier integration cost

  J. Legal score-first TTT.
  - The protocol across PR #549, #1413, #1493, #1148 is identical: 32K-token
  chunks, score each chunk under torch.inference_mode(), then SGD/Muon update
  the model on the already-scored chunk before moving to the next. Total eval
  time grows from ~80s sliding to ~480s.
  - Gain quoted:
    - SGD TTT (PR #549): вҲ’0.0025 BPB over sliding.
    - Muon TTT (PR #1148): вҲ’0.0037 BPB over sliding.
    - Entropy-adaptive Muon TTT (PR #1148): вҲ’0.0037 BPB, but concentrates budget
   on hard chunks.
  - Implementation: ~150 lines for the TTT loop + hyperparameter config; reuses
  our existing zeropower_via_newtonschulz5.
  - Risk: 2026-04-30 hard deadline; testing eats compute. Our eval
  infrastructure would need a score-first two-phase loop. Adds ~400s to eval.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.003 to вҲ’0.004 BPB if the rest of the stack is
  still underperforming (bigger gain on weaker model).

  K. Coprime-stride multi-shard loader.
  - PR #1060 / PR #726 style. Walks multiple shards at coprime strides so
  consecutive batches come from different documents.
  - Gain: not isolable in the records but @clarkkev's PR #1218 credits it with
  part of the jump. "Zero step time overhead" per PR #1060.
  - Implementation: ~60 lines replacing DistributedTokenLoader. Risk low; shards
   are pre-tokenized, no training change.
  - Expected at our stack: вҲ’0.001 to вҲ’0.002 BPB. Worth bundling with tokenizer
  swap.

  Tier 4 вҖ” gatekept by 5-day budget or other constraints

  L. Scylla-class TokenMonster tokenizer (our own).
  - Would require: running TokenMonster vocab construction on FineWeb (hours to
  days of CPU), retokenizing all training shards (90 min), adjusting our loader
  to read non-SentencePiece token IDs with a LUT similar to load_tokenizer_luts.
   Upside: could match or surpass 0.9485. Downside: the setup time is real, and
  we might end up with a worse tokenizer than candidate.vocab вҖ” picking good
  TokenMonster training corpora/hyperparameters isn't free.
  - Expected at our stack: potentially вҲ’0.10 to вҲ’0.15 BPB, but only if execution
   works in <4 days.

  M. Hessian-aware SDClip (PR #1412 non-record).
  - Modulates SDClip k per row by Hessian diagonal importance with О»=0.175.
  Reported gain ~вҲ’0.001 BPB and it's a non-record (self-admitted too few seeds).
   Low priority.

  5. What not to carry over

  - Our SWA w=256 + 5 full is actually a differentiator at seq_len=4096. None of
   the top records use SWA during training вҖ” they run full-attention for the
  whole seq_len. Our SWA lowers step time enough to afford longer seq_len or
  more steps; keep it.
  - Bank QAT is also our differentiator. 3-seed data showed it doesn't hurt at
  our LR schedule (QAT only triggers at LR-scale < 0.15, giving ~560 steps).
  Upstream doesn't have this on bank weights вҖ” keep it and hope it stacks.
  - Don't revert QK-Gain 2.5 вҶ’ 1.5 (the older nanogpt default). The direction of
   travel is higher, not lower.
  - Don't add SmearGate back if we already removed it. PR #1218 explicitly
  removed it when going to SP4096 and it didn't hurt.
  - Don't adopt SGD-only TTT if we adopt TTT. Muon TTT (PR #1148) dominates SGD
  TTT on identical wallclock.

  6. Recommended 5-day plan

  Given 2026-04-30 cutoff and our current 1.1117:

  3. Swap LZMA for byte-shuffle + Brotli-11. Verify artifact size < 16MB.
  4. Bump muon_wd 0.04 вҶ’ 0.085, add embed_wd = 0.085.
  5. Bump mlp_mult 3 вҶ’ 4 if artifact fits.
  6. 3-seed run. Expected landing: 1.07вҖ“1.08 BPB.

  Day 3 вҖ” optimizer + architecture shims.

  8. Bump QK_GAIN_INIT 2.5 вҶ’ 5.0.
  9. Wire depth recurrence on layers 4,5 with activation at 50% of training.
  10. 3-seed run. Expected landing: 1.06вҖ“1.07 BPB.

  Day 4 вҖ” parallel residuals, optional.

  14. Final 3-seed run, submit.
