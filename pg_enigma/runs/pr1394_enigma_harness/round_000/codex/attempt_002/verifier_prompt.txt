      "excerpt": "# Search Strategy Catalog for Optimizing `train_gpt.py`\n\n## Goal\n\nOptimize an executable training program and its surrounding contract to the **lowest credible `val_bpb`** on a given dataset.\n\nFor this repo, that means treating `train_gpt.py` as the object to optimize, not any one stage folder. The script already:\n\n- supports a bring-your-own SentencePiece tokenizer via `TOKENIZER_PATH` and `VOCAB_SIZE`\n- computes tokenizer-aware `val_bpb`\n- logs quantized end-state metrics such as `final_int8_zlib_roundtrip_exact val_bpb`\n\nSo the legitimate search space includes:\n\n- training dynamics\n- checkpoint/export selection\n- quantization/deployment path\n- tokenizer/vocab choice\n- architecture or global stack changes\n\nThe mistake is to let one search surface dominate forever just because an earlier stage emphasized it.\n\n---\n\n## First correction: there is no permanent fixed cycle\n\nThere is **not** one permanent stage-3-like frozen cycle for the whole project.\n\nInstead, there are **temporary search cycles**.\n\n- Across the whole project, you may search tokenizer, vocab size, prequant path, post-quant path, architecture, and training stack.\n- Inside any one tournament, you freeze only the dimensions needed for a fair comparison.\n\nThat distinction matters:\n\n- **project-level search** can move the base\n- **cycle-level search** should only vary one class of causal question at a time\n\nIf you forget this, the search gets trapped in the worst of both worlds:\n\n- too local to discover a new frontier\n- too mixed to trust the rankings\n\n---\n\n## What a search strategy actually is\n\nA search strategy is not \"some mutations.\"\n\nA search strategy is a choice of:\n\n1. **unit of variation** - what is allowed to change\n2. **unit of comparison** - what counts as a fair control\n3. **dominant metric** - what decides survival\n4. **evidence contract** - what data must be collected\n5. **reset rule** - when to stop mutating and change strategy level\n\nIf any of those are vague, you are not searching. You are just generating edits.\n\n---\n\n## Search levels\n\n| Level | Main question | Typical things that change | Things that should stay frozen |\n| --- | --- | --- | --- |\n| **L0. Metric/lane** | What outcome are we actually optimizing right now? | prequant vs post-quant vs final exact vs size/latency tie-breaks | everything else |\n| **L1. Base contract** | Which executable base deserves deeper search? | tokenizer, vocab size, architecture, optimizer stack, dataset variant | within-base family logic |\n| **L2. Program family** | Which consequential mechanism class is real? | controller, schedule family, optimizer handoff, selector, branch policy, quant path family | chosen base contract |\n| **L3. Your job is not to be agreeable.

## Principles

1. Weak keeps are worse than strong drops.
2. If an idea is only local tuning, mark it `DROP` or `REWRITE`.
3. If an idea is promising but too small, rewrite it **up one search level**.
4. Score every candidate on:
   - consequence
   - novelty
   - falsifiability
   - lane integrity
   - implementation honesty

- child variants of unproven parents
- mixed-lane claims
- hidden compounds
- hand-wavy probes
- dishonest "catalog executable now" claims

- sharp probes and kill rules
- honest search-level reframes
- real differences from prior failed lines
