## Novel levers surfaced

### Recurrence Depth Curriculum (PR #1756, romeerp)

**What it is:** Replace fixed recurrence depth (depth=3, 2 loops) with a phased curriculum during training:
- Phase 1 (steps 0–1/3): depth=1 (single forward pass — no recurrence)
- Phase 2 (steps 1/3–2/3): depth=3 (standard — matches our current baseline)
- Phase 3 (final 1/3): depth=4 (one extra loop repeat)
- **Eval always at depth=4** (one extra recurrence pass vs. our current baseline)

**Mechanism:** Teaches the shared loop block as a progressive refinement operator. By seeing depth-1 first, the block learns to function as a stand-alone layer; escalating to depth-4 trains it as a recursive refiner; evaluating at the deepest trained depth extracts the maximum refinement signal.

**Evidence quality:** 3-seed mean 1.06505 vs. our 1.06549 (Δ = −0.00044). Std = 0.00081 (4× SOTA std). Seed-by-seed: 0 → 1.06417, 42 → 1.06520, 1234 → 1.06578. The pre-TTT base model also improves (mean 1.07772 vs #1736 pre-TTT), suggesting the curriculum is not purely TTT-absorbed.

**Lever class:** training-loss (changes training depth schedule). Not absorbed by TTT because it improves the base model before TTT runs.

**Risks:**
- Std is elevated (0.00081); seed 1234 is worse than our baseline (1.06578 > 1.06549). The win is real in mean but a single-seed run could go either way.
- One extra recurrence loop at eval costs ~33% more compute in the loop block during inference/TTT — may affect TTT throughput.
- "Depth=4" evaluation means the artifact runs one extra loop pass at quant/TTT time. Worth confirming this doesn't affect the 10-minute wall time constraint during the official submission.

**Estimated Δ-bucket:** −0.0004 to −0.001 bpb net (above TTT), depending on seed.

**Feasibility:** High. Env-var controlled (`TRAIN_LOOP_PHASE_DEPTHS=1,3,4`, `TRAIN_LOOP_PREWARM_DEPTHS=3,4`, `EVAL_LOOP_DEPTH=4`). No new dependencies. Builds directly on our #1736 baseline. Code is already in romeerp's PR.
