## Stroll

The kill-wins result is genuinely surprising and I shouldn't rush to settle on a story. Let me wander.

**Why might selectivity hurt at 200 steps?** Hypotheses:

1. **Bf16 quant noise on per-token dynamics.** This is the one I keep returning to. in_proj is a 2D weight, bf16-quantized. The full version computes (dt[t], B[t], C[t]) from in_proj at every timestep, then drives a 1024-step recurrence with those bf16 outputs. Quant error accumulates. The kill version's _B_const, _C_const are 1D → auto-fp32 → recurrence is fp32-clean. We *already* protect 1D SSM scalars (A_log, dt_bias, D_skip) for exactly this reason; we just never thought to extend it to the (dt, B, C) projection slice of in_proj because in_proj is 2D. **This is a concrete oversight in the standard SSM-quant protection scheme, and the writeup angle is much better than "selectivity hurts at short training".**

2. **Capacity collapse + too many channels.** Kill version's d_state=64 collapses to a scalar κ per head (8 effective channels). Full keeps 64 × 8 = 512. The kill wins, so >8 channels is at best useless at our regime. But this cuts the wrong direction for the selectivity story — the issue isn't selectivity per se, it's "too many parameters to fit in 5M tokens of training".

3. **Pure under-training.** 144 of 2192 in_proj output dims feed into per-token dynamics. At 200 × 24576 = 5M tokens those dims are under-fit. They contribute noise. Same predictions as #2.

4. **Init mismatch.** At init, kill ≈ full except (B, C) start near zero. Selectivity has to grow from zero. In 200 steps maybe it never does.

I think (1) is the most testable and the most interesting. (2)/(3)/(4) are mostly "short-budget effects" — fine but generic. (1) is a specific mechanism with a specific writeup payoff.

**Decisive cheap test for (1):** split Mamba-2's in_proj into in_proj_zx (z + x slices, bf16-OK) and in_proj_dyn ((dt, B, C) slices, fp32-protected via CONTROL_TENSOR_NAME_PATTERNS). Run full-selective Mamba-2 with this protection. Param math: 144 fp32 dims × 512 input × 4 bytes × 2 unique blocks ≈ 600 KB compressed delta. Comfortable under cap. Subagent task — code change, ~30 lines.
